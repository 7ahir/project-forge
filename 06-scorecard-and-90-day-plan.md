# Scorecard and 90-Day Plan

## Question This File Answers

How would I measure the product honestly, and how would I enter the role without thrashing the team?

## PM Skills Demonstrated

- `executive-onboarding-playbook`: structures the first 90 days as diagnose, validate, then act with evidence
- `altitude-horizon-framework`: keeps the entry plan at product-leadership altitude and ties it to context creation
- `roadmap-planning`: connects the operating scorecard back to roadmap consequence rather than passive reporting

## In One View

- Measure repeated workflow value, not curiosity.
- Track trust where developers actually feel it in edit, test, and review work.
- Treat team expansion as the bridge to enterprise value.
- Spend the first 90 days diagnosing before changing the system.

## Scorecard Principle

The scorecard should answer four questions:

1. Are developers getting repeated workflow value?
2. Is trust improving where it matters?
3. Are teams expanding usage?
4. Is the product creating a better enterprise business?

These are illustrative metrics and targets, not claimed company data.

## Operating Scorecard

| Metric group | Example measures | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Developer usage | Weekly active users, repeat workflow completion, time to first useful diff, session depth by workflow | Separates curiosity from habit |
| Workflow quality | Edit acceptance, debug success, test repair usefulness, PR-summary usefulness, review-ready rate | Measures task-level value |
| Trust signals | Abandonment after low-trust output, manual rollback frequency, file-reference accuracy, confidence feedback, source visibility usage | Shows whether the product is becoming safer to rely on |
| Team adoption | Pilot expansion rate, active teams, lead satisfaction, standard-use share | Shows whether usage is scaling beyond champions |
| Enterprise motion | Pilots with a clear value case, expansion rate, time from pilot to rollout decision | Keeps GTM anchored to product truth |
| Delivery system | Bet review cadence, eval coverage, launch-stage discipline | Ensures the PM system itself is working |

## Metric Rules

- Do not govern the product on signups alone.
- Do not let one aggregate number hide workflow-level truth.
- Do not use enterprise pipeline as proof of product-market fit.
- Do not let trust remain qualitative only; instrument it where possible.
- Do not use prompt count as a substitute for coding-assistant usefulness.

## Leadership Readout I Would Want Monthly

- Which assistant workflows are strengthening or weakening?
- Where is trust improving or regressing?
- Which pilots are expanding, stalling, or failing?
- Which roadmap bets changed because the evidence changed?

## Example Review Rhythm

| Cadence | Review focus |
|---|---|
| Weekly | Edit, debug, test, and PR usage shifts, trust regressions, experiment learnings |
| Monthly | Bet health, roadmap re-ranking, pilot quality |
| Quarterly | Wedge health, expansion quality, category position |

## 30-60-90 Day Plan

This section follows the logic of the local `executive-onboarding-playbook` and `altitude-horizon-framework` skills: diagnose first, validate second, act with evidence third.

## First 30 Days: Diagnose

Primary objective:
Build the body of evidence before changing the system.

What I would do:

- interview product, science, engineering, design, sales, success, and rollout stakeholders
- review current IDE, repo, terminal, and PR usage patterns plus existing evals
- identify where the org currently confuses model quality with product quality
- map the top coding-assistant bets and the top sources of edit, test, and review friction
- review active enterprise asks for roadmap distortion risk

Deliverables:

- category and workflow diagnosis memo
- current-state map of product metrics, evals, and pilot motion
- initial view of where trust breaks in live coding workflows

## Days 31-60: Validate

Primary objective:
Pressure-test the emerging conclusions and name the highest-leverage changes.

What I would do:

- reality-check the diagnosis with product leadership and cross-functional peers
- define the small set of coding-assistant workflow bets that deserve protected investment
- tighten the scorecard to a smaller number of trusted signals
- identify one flagship initiative and one or two supporting quick wins
- align GTM on what the product can credibly claim now versus later

Deliverables:

- validated strategy memo
- prioritized bet stack with rationale
- draft roadmap and flagship initiative brief

## Days 61-90: Act With Evidence

Primary objective:
Install the product system and start the first meaningful changes.

What I would do:

- launch the operating cadence across product, science, engineering, design, and GTM
- commit the first roadmap tranche with explicit hypotheses and review points
- start the flagship initiative with a named owner group and success measures
- align pilot design and enterprise messaging to the updated product truth
- communicate a clear context cascade so teams understand what matters and why

Deliverables:

- operating model in motion
- first strategic roadmap in use
- flagship initiative underway
- leadership update anchored in evidence rather than theater

## Anti-Patterns

### Acting on arrival

Changing the roadmap, org shape, or GTM story in the first two weeks looks decisive and usually lowers decision quality.

### Vanity-metric governance

High prompt volume or signups can coexist with weak repo context, weak diff quality, and weak expansion.

### Over-weighting one large buyer

Enterprise opportunity matters, but one loud account should not define the category strategy.
